# Internal stakeholder update template

> Audience: PMs, leads, execs — non-technical, time-poor, want the "so what?"
> Tone: plain language, 2-3 paragraphs, no implementation jargon.

---

**{{name}} is live.**

{{One paragraph on what's different for the user or team. Lead with the
change someone outside engineering can actually see. Not "we refactored X" —
"users now see Y when Z".}}

{{Second paragraph: why it matters. Tie to whatever the team cares about —
retention, conversion, fewer support tickets, faster shipping, less risk.
Numbers if you have them; honest "we'll see" if you don't.}}

{{Optional third paragraph: what's next or what to watch. One sentence.}}

---

**Tone notes (delete on generation):**
- No "leverages", "synergies", "robust", "best-in-class".
- No bullet lists — flowing sentences. Bullets feel like a status report.
- "We" not "the team". "Users" not "end-users".
- If you'd be embarrassed saying it out loud to a friend, rewrite it.
